# NYC Taxi Data – BI Analysis

This project is based on the NYC Taxi & Limousine Commission (TLC) dataset, focusing on Yellow Taxi trips from the first half of 2022.

The goal was to explore the data without a predefined problem and build a report that helps understand what is happening and supports better decision-making.

---

## Overview

The dataset includes around 19 million trips. Using Power BI, I built a report to analyze demand, revenue, trip behavior, and geographic distribution.

The focus was to keep the analysis clear and useful, not just visual.

## What was analyzed

- Demand over time
- Distribution of trips by location
- Revenue and fare breakdown
- Trip patterns (distance, duration, passengers)
- Payment types

---

## Tools and techniques

- Power BI
- Data modeling (star schema)
- DAX and time intelligence
- Field parameters
- Calculation groups
- Conditional formatting
- Role-level security

---

## Key insights

- Trips grow consistently until March and then stabilize in the following months.
- Most of the demand is concentrated in a few areas, especially the Yellow Zone.
- Trip patterns are stable, with an average of ~6 miles and ~16 minutes per trip.
- Credit card is the dominant payment method, showing strong digital adoption.
- A large portion of revenue comes from standard fares and airport-related trips.

---

## Why this matters

This kind of analysis can help:

- Understand demand patterns and seasonality
- Focus operations on high-demand areas
- Improve pricing and revenue strategies
- Monitor efficiency through trip behavior
